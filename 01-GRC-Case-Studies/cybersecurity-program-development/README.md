# Cybersecurity Program Development — CGT Inc. Case Study

> End-to-end cybersecurity program design for a gaming software company, covering organizational structure, risk management, policy framework, contingency planning, and governance — delivered as a 5-part consultant engagement.

---

##  Scenario

Computer Gaming Technologies Inc. (CGT) is a premier gaming and entertainment software developer, founded in 1992 and acquired by Software-4-All in 2005. The company operates from a centralized campus north of Atlanta, GA, with ~80 employees across Gaming Development, Business Services, and IT divisions.

CGT has a **mature, well-structured IT department** with centralized infrastructure, encrypted backups, VPN-only access, and strong physical security. However, the organization has **no formal cybersecurity department, no security policies, no risk management framework, and no incident response capability**.

**The challenge:** Design a complete cybersecurity program from the ground up — staffing, risk management, policies, contingency planning, and governance — for an organization where valuable intellectual property (game source code, development platforms) is the primary asset at risk.

##  What This Project Demonstrates

- Designed a **6-person cybersecurity unit** with GRC-based tiered structure (Define → Build → Operate) and recommended organizational placement
- Structured a **3-phase risk management program** with governance framework, risk assessment methodology, and continuous monitoring
- Developed a **3-tier policy portfolio** (EISP, ISSPs, SysSPs) with representative policy examples tailored to CGT's operations
- Built a **4-phase contingency planning program** with BIA, IR/DR/BC teams, and testing protocols
- Designed a **cybersecurity governance model** based on ITGI principles and NACD board-level practices

##  Frameworks & Standards

- **NIST CSF** — Risk management and controls
- **COBIT / ITGI** — IT governance framework
- **NACD Cyber-Risk Oversight** — Board-level governance practices
- **NIST SP 800-34** — Contingency Planning Guide
- **IDEAL Model** — Continuous improvement lifecycle

##  Key Findings

- **CGT's IT maturity creates a false sense of security** — strong infrastructure but zero governance, policy, or response capability
- **Intellectual property (game source code) is the crown jewel** — Rack 2 servers and development platforms are Tier 0 assets requiring 4-hour RTO
- **CISO should report to the COO, not the CIO** — independence from IT operations while maintaining alignment with development and testing workflows
- **Physical security is strong but digital resilience is absent** — 24/7 guards and CCTV but no SIEM, no IR plan, no vulnerability scanning
- **The no-WiFi/no-BYOD policy is effective short-term but not scalable** — needs formal policy documentation and future-proofing

##  Analytical Approach

This project treats cybersecurity program development as a **structured consulting engagement** — each deliverable builds on the previous one, progressing from organizational design through governance:

```
Team Structure → Risk Management → Policies → Contingency Planning → Governance
     (who)           (what risks)      (rules)      (when things fail)     (oversight)
```

Each deliverable is designed to be **presentable to executive leadership**, reflecting the advisory communication style expected in GRC consulting roles.

>  *This case study was developed as part of a cybersecurity program development course, applying multiple frameworks to a realistic organizational scenario.*
