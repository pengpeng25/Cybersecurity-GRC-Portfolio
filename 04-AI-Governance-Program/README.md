# AI Governance Program Portfolio

## Overview

This repository presents a comprehensive **AI Governance Program** developed for **SimulFlow S.r.l.**, a fictional B2B SaaS company headquartered in Milan, Italy.

The project simulates the responsibilities of a senior GRC analyst establishing an enterprise AI governance framework from the ground up — covering AI system inventory, risk assessment, risk treatment, vendor governance, policy development, executive reporting, and audit readiness.

Rather than focusing on AI development, this portfolio emphasizes **AI governance**, **risk management**, **third-party assurance**, and **audit traceability** across internationally recognized frameworks.

## Control Traceability Chain

Every risk in this portfolio is governed through a complete traceability chain:

```text
AI Asset (01)
    ↓
Threat Scenario + Risk Score (02)
    ↓
Risk Register + Treatment Plan (03)
    ↓
Control + Framework Requirement (10)
    ↓
Evidence Artifact (01–09)
    ↓
Executive Reporting + KRIs (08)
```

This reflects how mature GRC programs demonstrate both control implementation and control effectiveness during internal or external audits.

## Technical Foundation

The AI security knowledge underpinning this portfolio was developed through:

- Completion of the **TryHackMe AI Security learning path**, covering AI threat modelling, prompt injection (direct and indirect), jailbreaking, system prompt leakage, supply chain attacks, RAG security, data poisoning, vector and embedding weaknesses, and unbounded consumption
- **ISACA — Auditing Generative AI: Strategy, Analysis & Risk Mitigation**
- **Kennesaw State University — Implementing a Risk Management Framework**
- Independent research into EU AI Act, GDPR enforcement, and emerging AI governance regulation

This technical background directly informs the risk identification, control design, and threat-to-control mapping throughout the portfolio — particularly in [03-AI-Risk-Register](03-AI-Risk-Register.md) and [10-Assurance-Control-Mapping](10-Audit-Assurance.md).

## Portfolio Highlights

This project demonstrates the ability to:

- Design an enterprise AI governance program from the ground up
- Build and maintain a risk register with appetite statement, ownership, and treatment tracking
- Perform structured AI risk assessments using OWASP LLM Top 10 and STRIDE methodology
- Develop enterprise AI governance policies including acceptable use and prohibited behaviour controls
- Conduct third-party AI vendor assessments with supply chain and GDPR analysis
- Map controls across ISO/IEC 42001, NIST AI RMF, SOC 2, and ISO/IEC 27001
- Produce executive dashboards with KRIs and priority actions for executive decision-making
- Assess ISO/IEC 42001 readiness and define a phased improvement roadmap
- Create audit-ready documentation with end-to-end risk-to-evidence traceability
